=== Exercise 7.9 ===
PageRank is determined by who links TO your page, not where your page links to.
Outbound links determine where traffic flows OUT of your page.
They have no effect on how much traffic flows INTO your page.
Hence, adding or removing outbound links does not change your PageRank.

=== Exercise 7.11 ===
Simply counting inbound links ignores page quality:
  - A spam site might have 1000 inbound links from low-quality pages.
  - A reference paper might have 50 inbound links from high-impact journals.
Google PageRank is more reasonable because:
  1. It weights inbound links by the PageRank of the linking page.
  2. Being linked by an important page transfers more PageRank to you.
  3. This recursive definition captures the notion of "importance by association".
  4. The steady-state of the random surfer model naturally reflects this weighting.
